Classic Horror Films from Universal Studios

Frankenstein (11/21/1931)
This film was directed by James Whale adapted from the novel by Mary Shelley. This classic stared Colin Clive as Dr. Henry Frankenstein, Mae Clark as Elizabeth, Boris Karloff as the Monster, and Edward Van Sloan as Dr. Waldman.
Dracula (12/2/1931)
The film was directed by Tod Browning, based on the novel by Bram Stoker, and stared Bela Lugosi as Count Dracula, Helen Chandler as Miss Mina, David Manners as John Harker, Dwight Frye as Renfield, and Edward Van Sloan as Professor Van Helsing.
The Old Dark House (10/20/1932)
Directed by James Whale, this film stared, based on based on the 1927 novel Benighted by English novelist J.B. Priestley, and stared Boris Karloff as the Morgan the Butler, Ernest Thesiger as Horace Femm, and Charles Laughton as Sir William Porterhouse. It involves a group of strangers stranded by weather in an old manor house in rural Wales, inhabited by a decadent family of aristocrats who have problems with mental illness, arson, and murder.
The Mummy (12/22/1932)
Directed by Karl Freund this film stared Boris Karloff as Imhotep (the mummy), Zita Johann as Helen Grosvenor, David Manners as Frank Whemple, Arthur Byron as Sir Joseph Whemple and Edward Van Sloan as Professor Muller.
The Invisible Man (11/13/1933)
Directed by James Whale, this film stared Claude Rains as The Invisible Man, Gloria Stuart as Flora Cranley, William Harrigan as Dr. Arthur Kemp, and Henry Travers as Dr. Cranley.
The Ghoul (11/25/1933)
Directed by T. Hayes Hunter, this film stared Boris Karloff as Professor Henry Morlant, an egyptologist who attempts to cheat death by being buried with an anceint amulet, and Ernest Thesiger as Laing.
The Bride of Frankenstein (4/22/1935)
This films was directed by James Whale and stared Boris Karloff as The Monster, Colin Clive as Dr. Henry Frankenstein, Valerie Hobson as Elizabeth, Ernest Thesiger as Dr. Pretorius, and Elsa Lanchester as The Bride.
The Werewolf of London (5/13/1935)
This is the first film about werewolves, and was directed by Stuart Walker, and stared Henry Hull as Dr. Glendon (the Werewolf), Warner Oland as Dr. Yogami, Valerie Hobson as Lisa Glendon, Lester Matthews as Paul Ames, and Lawrence Grant as Sir Thomas Forsythe.
The Wolf Man (12/12/1941)
This better known werewolf movie was directed by George Waggner stared Lon Chaney Jr. as Larry Talbot (The Wolf Man), Claude Rains as Sir John Talbot, Ralph Bellamy as Colonel Montford, Warren William as Dr. Lloyd, Patric Knowles as Frank Andrews, and Bela Lugosi as Bela the Gypsy.
The Creature from the Black Lagoon (3/5/1954)
This film was directed by Jack Arnold and filmed at Silver Springs State Park in Ocala, Florida. It stared Richard Carlson as Dr. David Reed, Julie Adams as Kay Lawrence, Richard Denning as Dr. Mark Williams Antonio Moreno as Carl Maia, Nestor Paiva as Captain Lucas, Whit Bissell as Dr. Edwin Thompson, Bernie Gozier as Zako, and both Ricou Browning & Ben Chapman as the Gill Man (creature).
